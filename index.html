02 What it does

Six things, done honestly.

/01

Live NCBI records

Every gene record, mRNA transcript, and accession is pulled live from NCBI's E-utilities. No stale dumps, no copies. What you see is what NCBI returns this minute.

RefSeq / GenBank
/02

DNA translation

Paste a coding sequence, get the protein. Full standard genetic code, GC content, stop-codon detection, start-Met recognition, three-letter and one-letter outputs.

A · T · G · C
/03

Mutation analysis

Compare two sequences. See substitutions, insertions, and deletions per position. Amino acid changes flagged as missense, synonymous, or stop-gained — with effect labels.

Genomics is a discipline drowning in its own evidence. NCBI alone holds billions of sequence records — carefully numbered, formally annotated, and almost completely unreadable to anyone who hasn't already learned to read them.

"The data is open. The vocabulary is not."

BioAI is a small attempt to bridge that gap. It pulls real records from NCBI — the same ones a research lab would query — and renders them as something a curious undergraduate can actually engage with. A gene becomes a paragraph. A mutation becomes a story. A coding sequence becomes a protein you can hold in your head.

Nothing is invented. Nothing is summarised away. The accession numbers stay where they are, and you can always click through to the original. The translation layer is just that — a layer.

Five model organisms to start. More if it turns out anyone needs them. No accounts, no email collection, no upsells. Just data, made legible.
